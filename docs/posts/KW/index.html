This chart contains every team’s 3 point shooting percentage from 2014-2015 up to the start of this season. During the early parts of last season when Maryland was struggling with their shot, there were times multiple times last season where Kevin Willard said that he will never tell his shooters to stop shooting 3s. Should he do that? All of the dots on this chart are where Kevin Willard’s teams have been in regards to three point shooting. The blue dots are from when he was at Seton Hall,and the two red dots represent his time so far at Maryland. All of Willard’s team are around the same in terms of their 3 point shooting percentage. This year, so far, however, has been an exception. Despite only being three games into the season, the Terps have been miserable from long range shooting just over 20%. It’s a number that one would expect to go up based on previous results, but so far, a big explanaition for the Terps lackluster start to the season has been their three point shooting.

Here is a chart with how Kevin Willard’s teams have fared rebounding the basketball. Maryland had a very small team last season which often resulted putting Julian Reese at the 5 for a lot of the time. At Seton Hall, Willard’s teams often rebounded the ball with great success, which was part of Willard’s succcess at Seton Hall. Last season was actually Kevin Willard’s worst season in terms of his team rebounding the ball since 2014-2015 and the Terps were still able to reach the second round of March Maddness. It makes one wonder how good a Maryland team could be if they could rebound the ball at the clip that some of Willard’s Seton Hall teams did it.
